PLANETS

Mercury

01

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. Its orbit around the Sun takes 87.97 Earth days, the shortest of all the planets in the Solar System.

ORBIT PERIOD: 87.97 Earth Days
KNOWN MOONS: None

Venus

02

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. As the second-brightest natural object in Earth's night sky after the Moon, Venus can cast shadows and can be, on rare occasion, visible to the naked eye in broad daylight.

ORBIT PERIOD: 87.97 Earth Days
KNOWN MOONS: None

Earth

03

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. About 29% of Earth's surface is land consisting of continents and islands. The remaining 71% is covered with water, mostly by oceans but also lakes, rivers and other fresh water, which together constitute the hydrosphere.

ORBIT PERIOD: 365.256363004 Earth Days
KNOWN MOONS: None

Mars

04

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, being larger than only Mercury. In English, Mars carries the name of the Roman god of war and is often referred to as the "Red Planet".

ORBIT PERIOD: 687 Earth Days
KNOWN MOONS: Phobos, Deimos

Jupiter

05

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass one-thousandth that of the Sun, but two-and-a-half times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined.

ORBIT PERIOD: 12 years
KNOWN MOONS: Europa, Io, Ganymede, Callisto, Amalthea, Himalia

Saturn

06

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter. It is a gas giant with an average radius of about nine times that of Earth. It only has one-eighth the average density of Earth; however, with its larger volume, Saturn is over 95 times more massive.

ORBIT PERIOD: 29 years
KNOWN MOONS: Titan, Enceladus, Tethys, Dione, Mimas, Iapetus, Rhea

Uranus

07

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. Its name is a reference to the Greek god of the sky, Uranus, who, according to Greek mythology, was the grandfather of Zeus and father of Cronus. It has the third-largest planetary radius and fourth-largest planetary mass in the Solar System.

ORBIT PERIOD: 84 years
KNOWN MOONS: Umbriel, Miranda, Titania, Oberon, Puck, Desdemona

Neptune

08

MNeptune is the eighth and farthest-known Solar planet from the Sun. In the Solar System, it is the fourth-largest planet by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet. It is 17 times the mass of Earth, slightly more massive than its near-twin Uranus.

ORBIT PERIOD: 165 years
KNOWN MOONS: Urbain Le Verrier, Johann Gottfried Galle, John Couch Adams
